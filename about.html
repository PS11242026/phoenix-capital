Methodology & evidence

How the portfolio is built

One signal, applied with discipline: a full DCF on every name in a validated universe, ranked by discount to intrinsic value and sized by how reliably that discount has predicted returns in that sector family. Everything below is measured by the same code that runs the portfolio.

The signal

Point-in-time fundamentals → conservative DCF → discount = intrinsic / price − 1. Discounts are clipped, ranked within validated sector families, and multiplied by a per-sector reliability score. Top-10 equal-weight; quarterly rebalance discipline; positions never leave the book between rebalances.

The evidence — stated honestly

US backtest (42 quarters, net of costs): alpha +4.3% /yr vs the S&P 500 at beta 1.431, +5.5% vs the Nasdaq 100. Caveat carried on every page: survivorship-biased universe; robust stats are long-short +15.6%/yr and top-N minus universe +7.4%/yr.

Two betas, both true

The strategy's 11-year backtest beta is ~1.43 — the average book it held. The current holdings' trailing-3y beta is ~0.51: a defensive, high-yield basket down ~40% from its peaks. That gap is the strategy: the signal buys after the damage. We label which beta every figure uses.

Operations

One command refreshes everything (prices → track → risk → site). News is fetched per holding from financial-news aggregation and labeled with its original outlet. Dashboard data is end-of-day pipeline output and upgrades to a direct live feed when a market-data key is configured. Nothing on this site is computed twice — pages render the same JSON the desk uses.

What would change our mind. The claim is scoped to the consumer/staples-family sectors where it survived corrected validation; if the live record decays past its historical drawdown envelope, the book gets cut, and the post explaining why stays up.
