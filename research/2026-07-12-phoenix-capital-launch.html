Phoenix Capital — why this portfolio exists

2026-07-12 · Phoenix Capital · launchmethodology

Phoenix Capital publishes one concentrated model portfolio built on one idea: **markets misprice

boring, forecastable cash flows more persistently than they misprice stories.**

The signal

Every name in a validated universe gets a full, conservative DCF from point-in-time

fundamentals. The gap between intrinsic value and market price — the discount — is ranked

within sector families and weighted by how reliably that family's discounts have predicted

forward returns. The ten widest, most reliable gaps form the portfolio, equal-weight.

The evidence, stated the way we'd want it stated to us

  • - Backtest, 42 quarters, net of costs: +4.3%/yr alpha vs the S&P 500 (beta 1.43),

+5.6%/yr vs the Nasdaq 100.

  • - The caveat we repeat on every page: the backtest universe is survivorship-biased. The robust

statistics are the long-short spread (+15.6%/yr) and top-10 minus universe (+7.4%/yr).

  • - The live record is weeks old. We publish it anyway, daily, because a track record you can

watch accrue is worth more than a backtest you have to trust.

The rules

  1. 1. Positions change only at scheduled rebalances, with the rationale published the same day.
  2. 2. Posts are never edited after publication; corrections get their own dated post.
  3. 3. Every figure carries its basis: backtest vs live, which benchmark, which beta.
  4. 4. If the live record decays past its historical drawdown envelope, the portfolio gets cut —

and the post explaining why stays up.

Membership buys research access, not portfolio management. That distinction is what keeps

this work independent — and what lets us publish the ugly weeks with the good ones.
