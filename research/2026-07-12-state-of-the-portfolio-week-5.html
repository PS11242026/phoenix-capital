State of the portfolio — week 5

2026-07-12 · Phoenix Capital · portfoliobenchmarks

Leading, but read it correctly

Five and a half weeks in, the portfolio is +7.3% against the S&P 500 at −0.3% — a

+7.6pp active gap. Before anyone annualizes that: the daily win-rate vs the index is only

~50%. The lead is a few names re-rating hard — QNST (+42% since entry), FLO, YELP, CAG — not

broad daily dominance. That is exactly the shape this signal's edge takes: long stretches of

nothing, then discrete re-ratings when the market closes a gap.

Against every comparable we could find

One benchmark is a choice; a panel is a test. Over the same window we track the portfolio

against the S&P 500, the Nasdaq 100, the Russell 2000 (closer to our market-cap mix), the

Russell 1000 Value (our factor neighborhood), Consumer Staples (our heaviest sector), and

Berkshire Hathaway (the flagship value manager anyone can buy instead of us). The dashboard

shows the full panel; if we ever start quoting only the flattering line, hold it against us.

What the current book actually is

Defensive: trailing-3y beta ~0.5, portfolio dividend yield ~3.9%, names down ~40% from their

peaks. The signal buys after the damage. The strategy's backtest beta is 1.43 — the average

book across 11 years. Two betas, both true; every figure on this site says which one it uses.

Watch items into the next rebalance: staples margins against private-label pressure (KR, CAG,

FLO), and whether the internet-marketplace re-ratings (QNST, YELP) have run ahead of their

intrinsic-value gaps.
